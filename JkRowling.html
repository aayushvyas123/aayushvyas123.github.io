She created the world's most famous, bestselling and multi-awarded book series Harry Potter with the marvellous art of her writing. You may have read it too. I'm a big fan of it too.
But not many people know what happened to her before she reached stardom and what situation she went through. So let's talk a little bit about that.

Joanne Kathleen Rowling is a British writer, screenwriter, producer and philanthropist, born July 31, 1965 in Yate, United Kingdom. She loved to write stories from an early age. She used to write strange stories from childhood and tell them to her sister. Rowling became a big fan of Jessica Mitford after reading she's autobiography, And she reads all her books.

In 1982, Rowling took the Oxford University entrance exam, but she wasn't selected.So she was forced to take admission Exeter University. After graduating from this university, She went to college to study French and graduated in 1986, after which she worked as a secretary in a company called Amnesty International, and later in Manchester, where she also worked at Chamber of Commerce. Her workplace was also very insulting to her.

While she was traveling by train from Manchester to London, the story of a young boy studying in a school of magic came to her mind, And she plotted the whole story in her mind, And when she arrived at her campus in London, she immediately started writing on it.However, her mother, who was closest to rowling, died a few days later.The incident also had an impact on Rowling's writing. And she was drowning in grief. Rowling avoided writing for a while due to this shock.

Then she resorted to writing to forget her grief, And began to spend all her time in writing. After a while she went to Portugal in search of a job, where she got a job teaching English. She worked at night and wrote during the day.In Portugal she met journalist Jorge Arantes. The two had a lot in common. So they were married on October 16, 1992, and had a daughter, Jessica, on July 27, 1993. But almost 13 months after the marriage, Rowling divorced.

Rowling then moved with her daughter to her sister to live there in Scotland. Until then she had written 3 chapters of Harry Potter. This time was too bad for rowling. Her marriage had failed, she had the responsibility of having a child, and she had no job. She filled out a form for welfare benefits to cover her expenses. Under which the British government paid for the house expenses of poor as well as single parents.

However, despite all these difficulties, she never stopped writing. And finally her first book, Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone, was completed. However, it took her about 5 years to write this book.She then took it to the publishers to get it published and there too she found failure. 12 The publishing house refused to print her book. But a year later, Bloomsbury, a publishing house in London, agreed to publish the book.

As soon as the book was published on June 26, 1997, it felt like a miracle. This book was very much liked by the people. The fourth novel, Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire, launched on July 8, 2000, broke all records. And 372,755 copies of this book were sold in the UK on the first day.

The fifth book in the Harry Potter series was released in 2003, and in 2004 Rowling was the first author to become a billionaire. This was followed by the publication of the seventh and final book on July 21, 2007, which broke all records and became the best-selling books all the time.
