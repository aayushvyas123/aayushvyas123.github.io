Bhagat Singh

  • Posted on, September 28, 2020

Today I am going to talk about the immortal martyr Bhagat Singh. Today, on his 113th birth anniversary, I pay a small tribute to him in the form of this blog. Bhagat Singh was an adventurer as well as a fickle, heroic and revolutionary one.

He was born on September 28, 1907 in Banga, Lyallpur (now Faisalabad, Pakistan) district in India. His father was Sardar Kishan Singh Sandhu and mother was Vidyavati Kaur.His father and uncle were also a Freedom fighter. He was against the British rule from his childhood. Instead of studying in a British school, he completed his studies in Dayanand Anglo Vedic School in Delhi. As he grew older, his sense of patriotism grew.

“They may kill me, but they cannot kill my ideas. They can crush my body, but they will not be able to crush my spirit"
-- Bhagat Singh.
