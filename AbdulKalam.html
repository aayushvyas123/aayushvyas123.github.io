A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

  • Posted on, July 24, 2020

Today I'm going to talk about a man who was not only a famous scientist but also the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007. He was honored with Bharat Ratna, Padma Vibhushan, Padma Bhushan and countless other titles.

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931 in Rameshwaram, Tamil Nadu. The entire life of the great scientist Abdul Kalam was dedicated to the service of the country and humanity. Abdul Kalam was an Engineer, Scientist, Author, Professor and Politician. From the beginning, his family's financial situation was not good at all. Kalam's father Jainulabiddin owned a ferry that took Hindu pilgrims back and forth between Rameswaram and Dhanushkodi. As Kalam's family was poor, he used to distribute newspapers and magazine.

“If you want to shine like a sun. First burn like a sun."
-- A. P. J. Abdul Kalam.

Kalam was an average student in school life but his passion for learning was strong. After completing his primary education in Rameshwar, he got admission in St. Joseph's College at Tiruchirappalli. Kalam graduated with a bachelor's degree in physics in 1954. In 1955, he was admitted to the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT) in Madras to study aerospace engineering. After graduating from the Madras Institute of Technology in 1907, he joined the Defense Research and Development Organization's (DRDO) aeronautical development institute as a scientist. Dissatisfied, he left the DRDO and joined the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO). He had served as the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) at ISRO. Kalam handled the job very well. After that, he never looked back, And one powerful missile after another gave to India.

He missed his dream of becoming a fighter pilot for just one place as there were eight positions available in the Indian Air Force while he was ranked ninth in the merit list. But Kalam never gave up and kept working hard And continued to do his duty for the country.

“Dreams is not what you see in sleep is the thing which doesn't let you sleep.”
-- A. P. J. Abdul Kalam.

Abdul Kalam played a major role in conducting the Pokhran-II, a series of five nuclear bomb test explosions in May 1998. With the success of these tests he got the status of a national hero and then Prime Minister Atal Bihari Vajpayee declared India a full-fledged nuclear state.

The Government of India awarded him the Padma Bhushan in 1981 and the Padma Vibhushan in 1990 for his work in ISRO and DRDO and for his services as a Science Advisor to the Government of India. In 1997, Abdul Kalam was honored with the Bharat Ratna, India's highest award for his contribution to scientific research and modernization of India's defense technology.

“Sometimes, it is better to bunk a class and enjoy with friends, because now, when I look back, marks never make me laugh, but memories do.”
-- A. P. J. Abdul Kalam.

Many good books have been written by Abdul Kalam, including India 2020, Wings Of Fire, Ignited Minds. One of them, in his book "India 2020", has strongly advocated for making India a knowledge-superpower and a developed nation. APJ Abdul Kalam was a leader who also contributed to youth skills and development initiatives.

Seeing Kalam's achievements as a wonderful scientist, Abdul Kalam was nominated for the presidency in 2002, And by 2007 he had done his job very well.

While addressing students at the Indian Institute of Management, Shillong , Kalam fell down, and died of a heart attack on July 27, 2015, at the age of 83. May this Noble & Precious Soul rest in Peace. Kalam was an ideal secular Indian, and read the Quran and the Bhagavad Gita with equal devotion every day. Indeed, a country like India is in dire need of such a man. Mr. Kalam always tried to enhance the pride of India. India will remember such a soul for myriad years.

“You can not change your future, but, you can change your habits and surely your habits will change your future”
-- A. P. J. Abdul Kalam.
