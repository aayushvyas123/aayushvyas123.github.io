Colonel Sanders

  • Posted on, July 15, 2020

Colonel Harland Sanders is well known for his world famous fast food restaurant chain Kentucky Fried Chicken, that specializes in fried chicken. The story of Colonel Sanders exemplifies his diligence, patience and wholehearted hard work for today's youth.

Although his life was full of tragedies and difficulties, he succeeded at the age of 65. So success and age are mutually exclusive. This quote from Mark Twain fits Sanders perfectly.

Colonel Harland David Sanders was an American businessman, born September 9, 1890 in on a little farm of Henryville, Indiana, USA.His father died tragically when he was only 6 years old. The situation at home then became such that the responsibility of his younger sister and younger brother fell on him at the young age of 7 years.

At the age of 10, Sanders took his first job at a local farm. His mother remarried when Sanders was 12 years old.Sanders' stepfather did not like having any children around him, so his younger brother was sent to live with his aunt and Sanders was sent to work on a farm 80 miles away in Greenwood,Indiana,USA. The job paid fifteen dollars a month and provided him with a place to sleep and something to eat.Initially, Sanders balanced his work at the farm and school.He would get up before dawn, feed the animals, attend school through the day and then come back in the evening to feed the chicken and perform other odd jobs around the farm. Sanders realized that he would work all day on the farm instead of going to school, so he dropped out of 7th grade.

For the next several years, Sanders did countless small and large jobs but failed each time. He also got married at the young age of 20 but then his wife left him.

In 1929, Sanders moved to Corbin,Kentucky,USA. where he opened a gas-station as well as a small restaurant next door where he made chicken for travelers. He tried to open another branches of his restaurant but he failed.

By the age of 65, he had done nothing more than to feed himself. He had a lot of positive influence from those who tasted the chicken. So he had full faith in his chicken recipe. He took spices and a Pressure Cooker and went out to market his unique method of making chicken. He visited every restaurant nearby and cooked his fried chicken on the spot for restaurant owners. Yet time did not accompany him. It was rejected 1,009 times before its chicken recipe was first approved.

After that, he never looked back. The first KFC franchise opened in the United States in 1952. As of early 2020, there are KFC outlets in 150 countries and territories in the world.
